About

Nicholas Maistros is a fiction writer whose work has been published in The Literary Review, Bellingham Review, Beloit Fiction Journal, Nimrod International Journal of Prose and Poetry, Sycamore Review, Sequestrum, Washington Square, and Witness. He holds an MFA in creative writing from Colorado State University, where he taught courses in literature, composition, drama, and creative writing. He has written two novels.

Nicholas has worked in nonprofit finance, Broadway merchandising, and food service. He has lived all over the United States, from New York City to Seattle. From Lubbock, Texas, to Ocean Springs, Mississippi, to Guam. He currently resides in Dayton, Ohio, with his partner. Together they founded Hidden City Publishing & Bookstore (at the moment a dream; soon to be reality).

Desperate Living Creatures: a Novel

Part ghost story, part meditation on self-sacrifice, Desperate Living Creatures is a tautly plotted character study that considers those moments in which we commit ourselves to the aid of others, and those when, should the cost prove too high, we look away.

They all wonder what brought Addy Pruitt, the darling of Dustwick, Missouri, to 147th Street. Particularly now, when the city’s smog level enshrouds the peaks of its buildings, when climate refugees have erected camps in its parks and subways, and when police watchtowers monitor its citizens’ movements from every street corner. They wonder the same thing about themselves, a group of thirty-something performers making small livings instead of art and gathering in bars and apartments to distract each other from their inhibited progress.

But there’s something different about Addy. She’s not here to pursue a career as a singer or a filmmaker, and she quickly strays from the sidelines on which the performers stand—she walks up to police towers to ask why they shine lights in people’s windows; she visits tent cities to talk to refugees; even as her pregnancy is becoming more apparent—and where is the father?—she organizes protests that end in teargas and arrest. Who is this person, they ask themselves.

Julian thinks he knows. Suffering from a degenerative disease and haunted by the ghost of his troubled and often violent brother, who may or may not be dead, Julian observes Addy from his window across the courtyard. He is certain that, like him, she is running from something dark. And when her past follows her to the city, and the performers are too distracted to notice, it is Julian’s consequential action, or inaction, that will determine Addy’s fate.

These Imaginary Acts: a Novel

This is the story of Raymond Meeks, who projects OMNIMAX movies at the Cincinnati Museum for twelve dollars an hour. He watches the film spool and unspool and wonders what went wrong. Why after ten years of traveling the country and making movies is he living once again in the forgotten factory town of his youth? Why, at thirty, is he renting a bedroom from his best friend, whose wife wants nothing more than for Meeks to disappear? Why couldn’t his life resemble the heroic subject of the museum’s latest film, Major A.B. Rogers?

This is also the story of Major A.B. Rogers, the eccentric nineteenth-century surveyor who discovers a deadly mountain pass in the Canadian Rockies. His nephew, Albert, has always idolized his uncle, but when he accompanies the scrawny but intimidating pathfinder on his latest adventure, young Albert learns just what—and whom—his uncle is willing to sacrifice to set his name upon the ages.

Projecting this film, day after day, Meeks decides that greatness does not have to contain itself in the postcard frames of history. So what if Kelly Sutphin, his oldest friend, is no longer the boy Meeks once adored? So what if Kelly gave up his dreams of being an Olympic diver to manage the pharmacy of a noisy supermarket chain, argue with his wife, Dee, and blow off steam at a gun range? Mountains can still be conquered. Tracks can still be laid. And if Kelly has lost sight of his own ambitions, maybe it’s time for Raymond Meeks to take his place, in whatever way he can.

An excerpt from These Imaginary Acts has been published in Washington Square's Winter/Spring 2015 issue.

All the Crowned Ruffians That Ever Lived: a Novella

A modern American fable, this is the rags-to-riches story of billionaire Jonathan Keating and the friend he left behind in the shuttered factory town of their youth. When they reunite at Keating’s island mansion, a reconciliation of their disparate pasts ends in tragedy.
