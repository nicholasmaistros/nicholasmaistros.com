About

Nicholas Maistros is a fiction writer. His work can be found in Witness, The Literary Review, Bellingham Review, Beloit Fiction Journal, Nimrod International Journal of Prose and Poetry, Sycamore Review, Sequestrum, Washington Square, and Longleaf Review. He holds an MFA in creative writing from Colorado State University, where he taught courses in literature, composition, and drama. He has written two novels.

Nicholas has worked in Broadway merchandising, nonprofit finance, filmmaking, and food service. He also served as co-editor for the first two issues of Palooka. He's lived all over the United States, from New York City to Seattle. From Lubbock, Texas, to Ocean Springs, Mississippi, to Guam. He currently resides in Dayton, Ohio, with his partner. Together they founded Hidden City Publishing & Bookstore (at the moment a dream; soon to be reality).

Projects

Desperate Living Creatures: a Novel

A literary ghost story, Desperate Living Creatures explores the tension between self-sacrifice and self-preservation. With elements of Andrew Holleran, Shirley Jackson, Don Carpenter, and Tony Kushner, it’s a novel that asks us to consider the moments we come to the aid of others, and when, should the costs prove too high, we look away.

Julian is hiding. From the degenerative illness that progressively claims his body. From the people of his hometown who’d pegged him and his brother, Mac, as pariahs. From the crimes he and his brother committed together after they’d abandoned their mother in that small Colorado town. And eventually, from Mac himself, who may or may not be dead, and whose ghost now haunts him in his New York City apartment.

Though he distracts himself with a fame-hungry group of comedians and opera singers, and though he tries to open up to his roommate and sometimes lover, Devon, it isn’t until Addy Pruitt, the darling of Dustwick, Missouri, takes the room across the courtyard-alley from his window that he finds someone who might understand.

Addy is hiding as well, Julian is sure of it. As their friendship grows, as he gleans more of Addy’s own troubling past, the city around them is having nightmares. Police watchtowers monitor citizens from every street corner. Smog enshrouds the peaks of its buildings. Climate refugees erect camps in its parks and subways. And when Addy’s past follows her to this choking city, it is Julian’s consequential action, or inaction, that will determine their fate.

These Imaginary Acts: a Novel

This is the story of Raymond Meeks, who projects OMNIMAX movies at the Cincinnati Museum for twelve dollars an hour. He watches the film spool and unspool and wonders what went wrong. Why after ten years of traveling the country and making movies is he living once again in the forgotten factory town of his youth? Why, at thirty, is he renting a bedroom from his best friend, whose wife wants nothing more than for Meeks to disappear? Why couldn’t his life resemble the heroic subject of the museum’s latest film, Major A.B. Rogers?

This is also the story of Major A.B. Rogers, the eccentric nineteenth-century surveyor who discovers a deadly mountain pass in the Canadian Rockies. His nephew, Albert, has always idolized his uncle, but when he accompanies the scrawny but intimidating pathfinder on his latest adventure, young Albert learns just what—and whom—his uncle is willing to sacrifice to set his name upon the ages.

Projecting this film, day after day, Meeks decides that greatness does not have to contain itself in the postcard frames of history. So what if Kelly Sutphin, his oldest friend, is no longer the boy Meeks once adored? So what if Kelly gave up his dreams of being an Olympic diver to manage the pharmacy of a noisy supermarket chain, argue with his wife, Dee, and blow off steam at a gun range? Mountains can still be conquered. Tracks can still be laid. And if Kelly has lost sight of his own ambitions, maybe it’s time for Raymond Meeks to take his place, in whatever way he can.

An excerpt from These Imaginary Acts has been published in Washington Square's Winter/Spring 2015 issue.

All the Crowned Ruffians That Ever Lived: a Novella

A modern American fable, this is the rags-to-riches story of billionaire Jonathan Keating and the friend he left behind in the shuttered factory town of their youth. When they reunite at Keating’s island mansion, a reconciliation of their disparate pasts ends in tragedy.
